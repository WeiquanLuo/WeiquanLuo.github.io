Milestone (Mar 8/2023): Now I understand why graduate-level statistics start from mean model.

The entire statistics world intend to answer one question: is there a difference between groups?

Different statistical measure, (so-called statistics) lead to different method (so-called hypothesis testing) and standardized measure (so-called effect size) By including co-variate, regression analysis by grouping (so-call random intercept) comes in. When variance is not uniform along the line (so-called violating homoscedasticity assumption), then generalized linear regression comes in. When the grouping has nested structure (usually cause by experiment design), interaction of the grouping and slope may exist (so-called random slope), then hierarchical Linear Regression and meta-analysis comes in.

So the reason why starting from mean model is that the regression is such an elegant framework for statistical communication from the most bottom to top method. However, people won’t understand why all are in regression language unless perceiving the initial intention of statistics.
